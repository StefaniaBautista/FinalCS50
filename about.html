Ley de Humedales = Wetland Law/Protection

As stated above, Ley de Humedales (this page's topic) stands for something like Wetland Law. It is not self-explanatory though: a wetland is kind of a hybrid ecosystem flooded by water where oxygen-free processes prevail. These are known as the most biologically diverse ecosystem of all. One of the most impressive things about them is their importance in climate change: they have mitigation effects through their ability to sink carbon, converting it to solid plant material through photosynthesis; and also through their ability to store and regulate water. Whatsoever, in some cases they also emmit nitrous oxide and methane, althought those emissions are really low in Argentina (related to the good they do).

Wetlands in Argentina take over more than 600.000km2, which is almost 21.5% of our territory.

They help us prevent floading, prevent climate change -as stated above-, and also letting us take care of the flora and fauna it grows.

For some cultural contexts: the argentinian government has historically had a lot of issues regarding "el campo" (as a mostly agricultural nation, those who work on this field have gained a lot of power because of the incomes the country recives thanks to their labor). People related to this field live by the countryside, normally far away from the biggest city in the country: Buenos Aires (CABA). Wetlands also happen to be far away from the city as well.

In 2020 the world had to face an almost unbreakable enemy: COVID-19. Argentina faces economic problems since any of us have memory. And, as any other country, our politic parties are really far away morally from one another. All of these factors got together last year:

On December 2019 we changed political parties (from republican to democrat), although both parties gained almost 50% each, neethless to say, our society has chosen two different paths. At the beginning of 2020 we knew about the coronavirus, but it had not affected us yet. On that moment, a known problematic was already starting to affect us: fires at the wetlands (like those the whole world saw at California). All of these started to grow a seed in every citizen, with a lot of hatred feelings towards each other, planting tension in our land.

When COVID started to approach us, everything went south. Some people were completely against the government, others only cared about the fires (that lasted for 8 months, even in national Tv they showed it counted times) because of the problems it would bring to our flora, fauna and economy. Anyway, with the lockdown (the largest of the world) Economy was already dying out there. People started to act as blind towards statistics or anything the government said.

With a lot of political changes we faced on 2020, out government did not even acknowledge the inmense fires. This is where the law takes action.

Wetlands' law have been taken to the Congress for 6 years in a row, althought it was born as a project in 2002. Even though it made it to the Congress last year, and the need to protect those lands is something indiscutible after all that happened, the political party have decided to not present it into votation and still has not put a date to it.

This is no new panorama for us argentinians, but, more than any other time, the need for this law is incredibly strong.
